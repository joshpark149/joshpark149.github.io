UBS Quant Hackathon 2020

The UBS Hackathon is an online quantitative strategy and financial data science competition hosted by the Alphien platform. This competition was to outperform the S&P 500 index by developing a long only portfolio using a payout function, which returns an equally weighted investment in 50 stocks.

The payout function takes in a pandas dataframe consisting of the close prices of stocks in the S&P 500 given a date, and it returns a pandas dataframe with dates we wish to rebalance and the desired weights for each company, which determines how much we invest. My methods truncate the dataframe of the close prices to only include our rebalancing dates. Then they fill each cell with a score, and then return a dataframe of allocations based on the highest scores on a given date.

My first method was to simply compare a short SMA (simple moving average) with a long SMA. The larger the short SMA is proportional to the long SMA, the greater indicator that the stock's value is increasing. This method surprisingly gave consistent results around 14% annualized returns, as seen to the left.

My second method involved comparing the positive and negative directional indicators(+/-DI) using high and low prices to calculate the average true range. +DI indicates the presence of an uptrend, while -DI indicates the presence of a downtrend. I scored based on how much greater +DI was compared to -DI. This method was more volatile, but could reach up to 18% annualized returns in some cases.
